La Nostra Storia

Three Generations,
One Oven

In 1987, Nonno Salvatore shipped a hand-built brick oven from the hills outside Naples to a quiet corner of Brooklyn Heights. He fired it with chestnut wood, stretched the dough by hand, and served pizza exactly the way his father had taught him — simple, honest, and full of soul.

Today, that same oven still burns at the heart of FireStone. Our dough rests for seventy-two hours, our tomatoes come from the volcanic soil of San Marzano, and our mozzarella is made fresh daily. Nothing is rushed. Nothing is shortcut. Every pizza that leaves our oven carries the weight of tradition and the warmth of family.

Perché FireStone

Why Choose Us

Every detail, from the bricks in our oven to the flour in our dough, is chosen to honor a single idea: pizza the way it was meant to be.

900°F Wood-Fired Oven

Our handcrafted dome oven, imported from Naples, bakes every pizza in ninety seconds — sealing in flavor with a perfectly charred, leopard-spotted crust.

72-Hour Slow Fermentation

Our dough rests for three full days before it ever touches the oven, developing the deep flavor and airy, digestible crumb of true Neapolitan pizza.

Imported Italian Ingredients

San Marzano DOP tomatoes, Caputo 00 flour, fior di latte mozzarella and extra virgin olive oil from Puglia — from the regions that perfected them.

Family Recipes Since 1987

Every recipe has been passed down through three generations, refined by hand, and served with the same pride as the very first pizza out of our oven.

Made Fresh, Every Day

No freezers, no shortcuts. Our dough is hand-mixed each morning, our sauces simmer slowly, and our mozzarella is pulled fresh daily.

Award-Winning Craft

Named "Best Neapolitan Pizza in the City" three years running, our pizzaioli train for years to master the art of the perfect pie.

Il Nostro Chef

Meet Our Chef

Pizza is not fast food. It's slow food, made fast.

Executive Chef & Founder

Antonio Bellini

Antonio learned to stretch dough standing on a wooden crate beside his grandmother's oven in Naples. At nineteen, he apprenticed in pizzerias across Campania before bringing the family recipes — and the family oven — to Brooklyn Heights in 1987.

Nearly four decades later, Antonio still arrives before sunrise to mix the morning's dough by hand. "A good pizza," he says, "needs patience more than ingredients. Everything else follows."
